Purpose

AFCA Ready helps financial firms and consumers identify and correct procedural, governance, and fairness risks before they escalate into binding AFCA outcomes or reputational damage.

AFCA outcomes are shaped by fact patterns, conduct, and procedural fairness. Most adverse outcomes arise from process failure, weak reassessment practice, or poor evidence discipline — not from a lack of “arguments”.

Important: AFCA Ready does not provide rule summaries, templates, or instructions. Our work focuses on how matters are handled in practice — including timing, ownership of decisions, evidence handling, and procedural fairness — not how rules read in isolation.

AFCA Ready examines how matters unfold across time and process — not isolated facts or arguments.

What We Do

AFCA Readiness Diagnostics

Identification of procedural and fairness exposure by reviewing how a matter has been handled over time — including complaint progression, reassessment conduct, evidence discipline, and decision accountability.

Process Alignment Review

Examination of whether handling practices align with AFCA’s fairness-based expectations in practice, including whether issues were properly reconsidered, explained, and owned at each stage.

Engagement Support (Non-Representative)

Assistance with organisation and clarity of materials during AFCA engagement. No submissions, arguments, advocacy, or representation are provided.

Deliverables

AFCA Readiness Snapshot

A fixed-scope diagnostic intended to surface handling risks early, based on the specific materials reviewed and how the matter has been handled to date.

  • 1–2 page written snapshot (Red / Amber / Green exposure indicators)
  • Procedural risks observed (timeline, reassessment, evidence handling)
  • Fairness alignment gaps where the record does not support the handling position

Scope boundary: This is diagnostic and process-support work only. No legal advice, financial advice, representation, advocacy, or claims management services are provided.

FAQ

Can’t someone just paste this into an AI tool?

Anyone can copy information. The value of AFCA readiness is not information — it is judgement about how the record reads in practice across time, what is missing, and where process integrity breaks down under scrutiny. Automated tools do not reliably assess procedural fairness in real-world files.

Do you tell people what to say to AFCA?

No. AFCA Ready does not provide submissions, arguments, or advocacy. We support evidence discipline, organisation, and clarity of materials only.

Who is this for?

Consumers seeking clarity before lodging or escalating a complaint, and firms seeking to reduce avoidable AFCA exposure driven by process failure.
